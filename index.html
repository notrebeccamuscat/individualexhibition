What is design theory?

Design theory is a system for helping graphic designers better understand how to communicate messages to audiences through visuals. It involves identifying elements in an image and explaining why they are important. Design theory can help designers make aesthetic decisions and ensure that their work communicates the intended message to viewers.

Graphic designers often employ design theory when creating images for marketing purposes, such as advertisements or brand logos.

Why is design theory important?

Design theory is integral to graphic design as it helps designers craft thoughtful and clear messages to viewers. For instance, if one wanted to highlight the importance of education, they could create a visual featuring children sitting at desks and interacting positively with their teacher. Incorporating design theory into advertisements and logos, for example, can help them grab viewers' attention and establish an emotional connection.

The Principles of Design Theory

There are five principles of design theory that individuals may use when beginning a new project. While each principle offers individual support to a project, it's important to have all five principles so that they can combine to communicate a clear and meaningful message to viewers.
